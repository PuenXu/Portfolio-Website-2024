My Story

Born in a medical doctor's family in China, I've never imagined to fall in love with something else, other than following my parent's footsteps and become a doctor like them.

The turning point came when I took an course in Robotics at WPI. I was amazed by this concept of odometry that uses simple math equations to determine the robot's movement. Before this course, math often seemed disconnected from practical applications, but now, robotics enabled me to reason about real-world phenomena using math equations.

Among all the intriguing topics in Robotics, Controls fascinated me the most. I enjoy employing different control techniques to enable physical systems execute desired behaviors. My research interest lies in Control of Underactuated Robotics, which includes Humanoids and Quadrupeds. To prepare myself to contribute to the research field, I am taking online courses (e.g. Optimal Control and Reinforcement Learning at CMU) to learn latest techniques in the Control area.

Please feel free to contact me for potential collabration. I am open to a summer research internship and industry experiences.

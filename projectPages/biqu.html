Robotic Arm Design for Quadruped Robot Loco-Manipulation

Objective

In the growing field of legged robotics, the integration of locomotion and manipulation poses a significant challenge. The 23-24 BiQu MQP Team at WPI presents an open-source framework that facilitates planning through contact, enhancing robots’ capabilities in logistics, construction, and even planetary exploration. To validate the framework’s effectiveness, it is tested on a Unitree Go1 quadruped robot equipped with a custom robotic arm.

My Contributon

  • Designed, manufactured, and built a light and robust 3-DoF robotic arm with 2-DoF Gripper using SolidWorks as well as a rail system to attach the robot manipulator onto the Unitree Go1 Edu Quadruped Robot.
  • Developed a ROS-Serial interface that allows communication between the robotic arm (Arduino) and the Unitree Go1 robot (Raspberry Pi).
  • Programmed and tuned a PD velocity controller in Arduino to achieve desired robotic arm motion (motor commands) with high precision.
  • Collaborated with teams to integrate the robotic arm into the in-house trajectory optimization solver, enabling loco-manipulation of the custom quadruped robot.
